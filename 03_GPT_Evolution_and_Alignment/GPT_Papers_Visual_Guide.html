Foundation
Attention Is All You Need
Vaswani et al. · Google Brain
Introduced the Transformer — the architecture that powers every paper on this page.
Self-Attention Encoder-Decoder Positional Encoding
Replaced RNNs with pure attention. The multi-head attention + feed-forward block is still unchanged in GPT-4, Claude, and Gemini today.
arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762 ↗
GPT-1 and BERT both take the Transformer — and split into two schools: decoder-only vs encoder-only
2018
GPT Series · 01
GPT-1 — Language Understanding by Generative Pre-Training
Radford et al. · OpenAI
First proof that pre-training a Transformer decoder on unlabelled text, then fine-tuning, beats task-specific training.
117M params BooksCorpus Pre-train + Fine-tune
12-layer decoder, trained on 7,000 books. Improved SOTA on 9 of 12 NLP benchmarks. Started the foundation-model era.
OpenAI PDF ↗
Encoder · 01
BERT — Bidirectional Transformers for Language Understanding
Devlin et al. · Google AI
Read text in both directions simultaneously using Masked Language Modelling — better for understanding, can't generate.
Bidirectional MLM + NSP Encoder-only
Set new SOTA on 11 NLP benchmarks. GLUE: 80.5%, SQuAD F1: 93.2%. Encoder-only means great at classification — but cannot write text. GPT eventually won the LLM race because generation > classification.
arxiv.org/abs/1810.04805 ↗
GPT-2 asks: what if we scale up and remove fine-tuning entirely?
2019
GPT Series · 02
GPT-2 — Unsupervised Multitask Learners
Radford et al. · OpenAI
Scale to 1.5B params and train on 40GB of web text — the model learns tasks zero-shot without any fine-tuning.
1.5B params WebText 40GB Zero-shot
SOTA zero-shot on 7 of 8 LM benchmarks. OpenAI initially withheld the full model — the first AI safety controversy. Showed the internet is a multitask dataset.
OpenAI PDF ↗
Encoder-Decoder · 01
BART — Denoising Seq2Seq Pre-training
Lewis et al. · Facebook AI
Combines BERT's bidirectional encoder with GPT's autoregressive decoder — best of both worlds for summarisation.
Encoder + Decoder Denoising Summarisation
Pre-trained by corrupting text (masking, shuffling, deletion) and learning to reconstruct it. ROUGE-1 44.16 on CNN/DailyMail — new SOTA. Still widely used for summarisation and translation.
arxiv.org/abs/1910.13461 ↗
GPT-3: massive scale makes few-shot examples in the prompt sufficient — no fine-tuning, no labels
2020
GPT Series · 03
GPT-3 — Language Models are Few-Shot Learners
Brown et al. (31 authors) · OpenAI
At 175B params, a few examples in the prompt (no weight updates) matches fine-tuned models — in-context learning emerges.
175B params 300B tokens Few-shot ICL ~$4.6M to train
No gradient updates needed for new tasks. Spawned prompt engineering as a field. The model behind the OpenAI API. Limitation: follows statistical patterns, not instructions — led directly to InstructGPT.
arxiv.org/abs/2005.14165 ↗
The alignment era begins — how do you make a text predictor into a helpful, harmless assistant?
2022
Alignment · 01
InstructGPT — Training LMs to Follow Instructions with Human Feedback
Ouyang et al. · OpenAI
RLHF in 3 steps: SFT → reward model → PPO. A 1.3B aligned model preferred over raw 175B GPT-3 by humans 85% of the time.
RLHF PPO → ChatGPT
Alignment ≠ capability. Smaller + aligned beats bigger + unaligned. This 3-step pipeline is exactly how ChatGPT was built. The "alignment tax" (slight benchmark regression) is a known trade-off.
arxiv.org/abs/2203.02155 ↗
Alignment · 02
HH-RLHF — Helpful & Harmless Assistant with RLHF
Bai et al. · Anthropic
Anthropic's RLHF paper — introduces the HHH framework and reveals the fundamental tension: more helpful often means more harmful.
HHH Framework ~170K pairs → Claude
Two separate reward models: one for helpfulness, one for harmlessness. Helpfulness ↑ often means harmlessness ↓ — you must manage the trade-off explicitly. The HH dataset is still widely used for research.
arxiv.org/abs/2204.05862 ↗
Alignment · 03
Constitutional AI — Harmlessness from AI Feedback
Bai et al. · Anthropic
Replace expensive human harmlessness labels with an AI critiquing its own outputs against a written constitution of principles.
SL-CAI RLAIF AI Feedback
Two-stage: (1) Generate → Critique → Revise loop (SL-CAI), (2) AI preference labels → reward model → PPO. The constitution is published — transparent values. Basis for all Claude models.
arxiv.org/abs/2212.08073 ↗
2023: the field simplifies — can we remove the reward model entirely? Can the model improve itself?
2023
Alignment · 04
RLAIF — Scaling RLHF with AI Feedback
Lee et al. · Google
Replace human raters with an LLM — RLAIF achieves comparable alignment to RLHF at a fraction of the cost and time.
AI Rater Scalable ≈ Human Quality
Win rates vs SFT baseline: RLAIF and RLHF approximately equal (~71–73%). Independently validates Anthropic's Constitutional AI approach. As LLMs improve, RLAIF quality improves automatically.
arxiv.org/abs/2309.00267 ↗
Alignment · 05
DPO — Direct Preference Optimization
Rafailov et al. · Stanford
The language model IS the reward model — skip PPO entirely and optimise directly on preference pairs with a simple binary cross-entropy loss.
No Reward Model No PPO Used in LLaMA / Mistral
Derives a closed-form solution to the RLHF objective: reward(x,y) ∝ log π(y|x)/π_ref(y|x). Matches or beats PPO on summarisation, dialogue, sentiment. Now the default for open-source fine-tuning.
arxiv.org/abs/2305.18290 ↗
Alignment · 06
SELF-REFINE — Iterative Refinement with Self-Feedback
Madaan et al. · CMU / AI2
No training needed — the same LLM generates, critiques its own output, and revises it in a loop, like a human reviewing a draft.
No Training Generate → Critique → Refine ~20% improvement
Tested on 7 tasks (code, math, dialogue, summarisation, etc.). Average ~20% relative improvement over initial generation using GPT-3.5. Foundation for agentic AI critic loops in LangGraph and CrewAI.
arxiv.org/abs/2303.17651 ↗
