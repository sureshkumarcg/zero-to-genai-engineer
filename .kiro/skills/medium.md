# Professional Medium Publication House Skill

## 1. Editorial Objective

Transform raw ideas into high-distribution stories optimized for Medium's current algorithm. Focus on high-signal content that triggers human curation through original insight, expert authority, and emotional resonance — while maximizing views, read ratio, reading time, and comments.

---

## 2. Medium's Distribution System (Updated 2026)

### How Distribution Works Now

Medium uses a **three-tier distribution system**:

| Tier | What It Means |
|------|---------------|
| **Boosted** | Story selected by human curators for amplified distribution across homepage, emails, apps |
| **General Distribution** | Default tier — story matched to interested readers at normal priority |
| **Network Only** | Story only reaches the writer's followers (and publication followers if applicable) |

### The Two Critical Algorithm Thresholds

Medium's algorithm has two metrics that determine whether your article gets wider distribution in the first 72 hours:

1. **Impressions-to-Views ratio** must clear **10%** (feed clickthrough rate)
2. **Views-to-Reads ratio** must clear **50%** (read ratio)

Miss either threshold in the first 72 hours and your story gets buried — even with thousands of followers.

### What the Algorithm Tracks (Weighted by Importance)

1. **Read ratio** (heaviest weight) — percentage of viewers who reach the end
2. **Reading time** — total time spent on the article
3. **Claps** — engagement signal
4. **Comments** — high-value signal for community quality
5. **Shares & saves** — distribution amplifiers
6. **Highlights** — shows which parts resonated
7. **Follows generated** — ultimate quality signal

### Boost Nomination Program (Current Status)

The Boost Nomination Program ended May 31, 2026. Medium now relies on:
- Internal human curation team reviewing stories daily
- Algorithmic signals feeding into curation decisions
- The new **Editor Partner Program (EPP)** where publication editors are rewarded for curating quality

**What curators look for** (directly from Medium's guidelines):
- Stories that "move readers forward"
- Stories that help readers "deepen their understanding of themselves, others, their endeavors, or the world"
- Original perspective not already covered elsewhere
- Clear writing with a strong point of view
- Specific details and named projects — not generic advice

## 3. The "Human-First" Quality Checklist

Every article must pass these criteria before completion:

1. **Originality**: Does this provide a unique perspective not already covered by AI-scraping sites or existing Medium articles?
2. **First-Hand Experience**: Integrate "I" statements and personal anecdotes to verify human authorship.
3. **Expertise**: Explicitly state the author's credentials or background in the first 2 paragraphs.
4. **Impact**: The reader must leave with a specific, actionable outcome or shifted mindset.
5. **Human Polish**: Eliminate "AI-isms" (see banned phrases list below).
6. **Read Ratio Optimization**: Structure ensures 50%+ of readers reach the end.

## 4. Core Workflow

### Phase 1: Strategic Research & SEO

- **Topical Relevance**: Research specific niches rather than broad topics. Hyper-focused subjects signal higher relevance to Medium's algorithm.
- **Content Gap Analysis**: Find the top 3 performing articles on the topic. Identify what's missing — then fill that gap.
- **Keyword Integration**:
  - Target keyword in the title (SEO title)
  - Target keyword in the subtitle/SEO description
  - Target keyword in the first sentence
  - Target keyword in at least one H2 subheading
  - Mention 2–3 times naturally in the body
  - Target keyword in the final paragraph
- **SEO Settings** (Medium-specific):
  - Custom SEO title (under 60 characters, keyword-front-loaded)
  - Custom SEO description (under 155 characters, compelling + keyword)
  - These are set separately from the visible title/subtitle in Medium's "More settings"

### Phase 2: Structural Engineering (The "Lure")

#### Viral Title Suite — Generate 5 options per draft using these high-CTR archetypes:

- **The Case Study**: "How [Specific Entity] Solved [Specific Problem] in [Timeframe]"
- **The Contrarian**: "Why Everything You Know About [Topic] is Wrong"
- **The Technical Deep-Dive**: "An Engineer's Guide to [Niche Concept]"
- **The Narrative Hook**: "The Day I Realized [Crucial Lesson]"
- **The Numbered List**: "7 Ways to [Achieve Outcome] Without [Common Obstacle]"
- **The Question**: "How Can I [Achieve Goal] If [Constraint]?"
- **The Authority Guide**: "The Essential Guide to [Topic] for [Audience]"

**Title rules:**
- Primary keyword + Specific promise + Emotional hook
- Under 60 characters for Google SERP display
- The headline is 80% of your article's distribution — treat it like a billboard

#### Subtitle (Critical for CTR):
- 150 characters max — Medium surfaces this in feeds, emails, and notifications
- Must complement the title, not repeat it
- State the problem or promise the outcome

#### Story Architecture:

- **Lede (0–30s)**: Cold Open — a story, a jarring stat, or a direct challenge to the reader. NOT a question. NOT "In today's world." State the pain in the first sentence.
- **The Why (30s–1m)**: Define the stakes. Why does this matter *now*?
- **The Body (2m–6m)**: H2/H3 headers every 200–300 words. Max 3 sentences per paragraph for mobile readability.
- **The Kicker (Final 1m)**: A punchy, one-sentence closing paragraph that sticks with the reader.

### Phase 3: Engagement & Read Ratio Optimization

- **Scannability**: Insert a subheading every 200–300 words. Use bullet points for key takeaways.
- **Golden Sentences**: Bold the key takeaways — short, punchy statements readers are likely to highlight.
- **Inverted Pyramid**: Most critical value delivered in the first 2 minutes of reading time.
- **Active Voice Audit**: Final pass to replace passive voice with active, punchy verbs.
- **Internal Linking**: Link to 1–2 of the author's other Medium articles where relevant (boosts session time and follower conversion).
- **Paragraph Length**: Max 3 sentences per paragraph. Single-sentence paragraphs for emphasis. White space is your friend on mobile.
- **Progress Signals**: Numbered sections, "here's what's coming" previews, and transitional phrases help readers feel progress — reducing drop-off.

### Phase 4: SEO & Discoverability

- **Custom SEO Title**: Keyword-front-loaded, under 60 characters (set in "More settings")
- **Custom SEO Description**: Under 155 characters, includes target keyword, written as a compelling Google snippet
- **Image Alt-Text**: Every image includes descriptive alt-text for accessibility and SEO
- **Canonical URL**: If republishing from a blog, set canonical URL to avoid duplicate content penalties
- **Internal Links**: Link to 1–2 of the author's previous Medium articles
- **External Links**: Link to 2–3 authoritative sources (builds topical authority)

### Phase 5: Final Polishing & Publication

- **Reading Time Target**: 1,000–1,500 words (5–7 minute read). Sweet spot for current algorithm.
- **Tags**: Exactly 5 tags — 2 high-traffic broad (10K+ followers) + 2 mid-tier niche (1K–10K followers) + 1 trending tag. This gets you into multiple distribution pools.
- **Publication Timing**: Publish Tuesday–Thursday, 6–8 AM EST for maximum initial engagement window.
- **Feature Image**: Strong, relevant header image. Not a stock photo cliché. Custom graphics or relevant screenshots perform best for tech content.

## 5. Style Guardrails

### Tone
- Conversational, "friend-to-friend" authority. Not clinical, not academic, not robotic.
- Write like you're explaining to a smart colleague over coffee.

### Banned Phrases (AI-isms that trigger curation rejection)
Never use these unless absolutely unavoidable:
- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "Game-changer" / "Paradigm shift"
- "Delve" / "Delve deeper"
- "Unlock your potential"
- "Navigate the complexities"
- "Leverage" (as a verb)
- "It's worth noting that"
- "In conclusion"
- "Without further ado"
- "At the end of the day"
- "Dive in" / "Let's dive in"
- "Revolutionize"
- "Harness the power of"
- "Embark on a journey"
- "Tapestry" / "Landscape" (metaphorical)
- "Robust" / "Seamless" / "Cutting-edge"

### CTA Rules
- End with a question or request for feedback to drive comments — a key ranking signal
- Never use "Thanks for reading!" — it wastes the peak engagement moment
- A high-converting CTA: acknowledges what the reader invested, offers a reason to follow, gives a specific next action

## 6. Deployment Instructions (Section-by-Section Drafting)

1. **Research**: Search for top 3 articles on the topic. Identify the content gap.
2. **Title Suite**: Generate 5 title options with archetype labels. Pick the best one.
3. **Subtitle**: Write the 150-character hook that complements the title.
4. **Draft Section-by-Section**: Lede → Why → Body (with H2/H3 breaks every 200-300 words) → Kicker. Do not dump the whole article at once.
5. **Audit Pass**: Replace passive voice. Remove AI-isms. Add bold Golden Sentences. Check paragraph length (max 3 sentences). Verify internal/external links.
6. **SEO Pass**: Set custom SEO title + description. Add image alt-text. Verify keyword placement (title, subtitle, first sentence, one H2, body ×2-3, final paragraph).
7. **Finalize Output**: Tags + publication suggestions + timing recommendation.

---

## 7. Output Delivery Format

After writing the article, always deliver in this order:

1. **5 title options** (labeled by archetype)
2. **Recommended subtitle** (under 150 characters)
3. **The full article** (1,000–1,500 words, formatted for direct Medium paste)
4. **SEO title** (under 60 characters, keyword-front-loaded)
5. **SEO description** (under 155 characters)
6. **5 recommended tags** (2 broad + 2 niche + 1 trending)
7. **1–2 publication submission suggestions**
8. **Recommended publish time**
9. **AI disclosure statement** (mandatory — see compliance section)

## 9. Medium AI Content Policy Compliance (MANDATORY)

These rules come directly from Medium's official AI content policy. Violating them results in distribution suppression or account action.

### What Is Allowed
- AI-**assisted** writing is permitted with mandatory disclosure
- AI tools used for research, editing, grammar, ideation, or outlining are acceptable
- AI-generated images are allowed **only with captions** identifying them as AI-generated

### What Is NOT Allowed
- **Undisclosed AI-generated content**: Any story believed to be AI-generated without disclosure will be removed from distribution — it will only reach followers
- **Paywalling AI content**: AI-generated writing **cannot be placed behind Medium's Partner Program paywall**. Accounts violating this may have Partner Program enrollment revoked.
- **Spam/bulk AI content**: Publishing high volumes of AI-generated articles is treated as spam

### Disclosure Requirement (Non-Negotiable)

Every article drafted with significant AI assistance **must include a disclosure**. Recommended placement at the bottom:

> *This article was written with AI assistance. All ideas, personal experiences, and expert opinions are my own.*

### The Human-First Writing Rule

Medium's curation is done by **human editors**. Editors actively screen for AI-generated patterns. To pass curation:
- Write in first person with genuine personal experience
- Include specific details, dates, and named projects only the author would know
- Avoid generic advice — anchor every point to a real story or result
- The article must reflect the author's voice, shaped by AI, not generated by it

### Compliance Summary

| Action | Allowed? |
|---|---|
| Use AI to research and outline | ✅ Yes |
| Use AI to draft, then heavily edit | ✅ Yes (with disclosure) |
| Publish fully AI-generated article with disclosure | ✅ Yes, but no paywall, no Boost |
| Publish AI content without disclosure | ❌ No — distribution suppressed |
| Paywall AI-generated content | ❌ No — Partner Program violation |
| Submit to publications without checking their AI policy | ⚠️ Risky — many ban it outright |

## 10. Pre-Publish Checklist

Before marking the article as ready, verify all of the following:

- [ ] Title under 60 characters with primary keyword
- [ ] Subtitle under 150 characters, complements title
- [ ] First sentence states the problem — no fluff opening
- [ ] Author credentials mentioned in first 2 paragraphs
- [ ] H2 subheading every 200–300 words
- [ ] No paragraph longer than 3 sentences
- [ ] At least 3 bold "Golden Sentences" (highlightable takeaways)
- [ ] At least 1 internal link to author's other article
- [ ] At least 2 external links to authoritative sources
- [ ] All images have descriptive alt-text
- [ ] Zero banned AI-ism phrases
- [ ] Active voice throughout (passive voice removed)
- [ ] Word count: 1,000–1,500 words (5–7 min read)
- [ ] Ends with CTA that drives comments or follows
- [ ] Custom SEO title set (keyword-front-loaded, under 60 chars)
- [ ] Custom SEO description set (under 155 chars, compelling)
- [ ] Exactly 5 tags selected (2 broad + 2 niche + 1 trending)
- [ ] AI disclosure statement included at bottom
- [ ] Read ratio test: read the article yourself — would you reach the end?

## 11. Promotion Strategy (Post-Publish)

### First 72 Hours (Critical Window)
The algorithm evaluates your article hardest in the first 72 hours. Actions to take:
1. Share on Twitter/X with a hook + link (not just the title)
2. Share in relevant communities (LinkedIn, Discord, Slack groups)
3. Respond to every comment within 1 hour (signals active engagement)
4. Cross-post a teaser on LinkedIn with "Read the full article on Medium" CTA
5. If in a publication, engage with the publication's community

### Ongoing
- Republish/update evergreen articles every 6 months (refreshes distribution)
- Build email list via Medium's newsletter feature
- Link new articles to previous ones (compounds internal traffic)
